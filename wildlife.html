Forest mapping

Drones can be used to create detailed maps of forested areas. Our services equipped with cameras and other sensors can collect high-resolution imagery, providing data to create detailed maps of the forest. These maps can be used for various purposes, such as monitoring changes in the forest over time, identifying areas of deforestation or illegal logging, and conducting research on the health and growth of the trees.

Topographic survey & boundary definition

Utilizing drones for topographical surveys enables rapid and precise mapping of land contours, elevations, and terrain features. High-resolution imagery and LiDAR data captured by drones facilitate accurate boundary demarcation and property delineation. This technology offers cost-effective and efficient alternatives to traditional surveying methods, reducing time and resource requirements. Detailed topographical data obtained from drone surveys aids in land development, infrastructure planning, and environmental management projects. Overall, drones streamline topographical surveys and boundary demarcation processes, providing valuable geospatial information for various applications.

Forest health mapping

Drones with high-resolution and thermal cameras can be used to closely evaluate individual trees for signs of disease or damage. This can help forest managers identify and handle possible issues before they spread. This information can then be used to create plans for preserving and increasing tree health, ultimately contributing to the preservation and protection of forest ecosystems.

Wildlife Monitoring

Drones can be used to monitor wildlife in forested areas, allowing researchers to see animal movements and behaviour without bothering them. This can provide crucial information about the health and well-being of many species. A drone allows researchers to acquire a bird's-eye perspective of the landscape and track animal movements across a broad area.

This is particularly beneficial for investigating animals that are difficult to trace on foot. Drones can also be equipped with cameras and other sensors to collect data on the behaviour of animals, monitor species numbers, and detect movement patterns.

  • Drones provide a non-intrusive method for observing elusive or nocturnal species, such as owls or bats, without disturbing their natural habitats.
  • By capturing high-resolution imagery and video footage, drones enable detailed analysis of animal interactions, feeding behaviors, and habitat preferences.
  • Real-time data streaming capabilities allow researchers to adapt their observation strategies on the fly, maximizing the efficiency of wildlife monitoring efforts.
  • Integration of advanced technologies like thermal imaging sensors enhances the ability to detect animals hidden beneath dense foliage or during low-light conditions, improving overall research accuracy.
Fire Detection and Management

Drones coupled with thermal imaging cameras provide rapid response and important insights into forest fire detection and management. They can track the spread of fires, assess damage, and offer real-time data to ground-based firefighters. Drones help suppress fire outbreaks and reduce their impact on forest ecosystems.

  • Rapidly detect and track the spread of forest fires using drones equipped with thermal imaging cameras, enabling early intervention and effective management strategies.
  • Provide real-time data on fire behavior, intensity, and direction to ground-based firefighters, facilitating informed decision-making and coordinated response efforts.
  • Assess the extent of fire damage and identify areas of concern within the forest ecosystem, guiding post-fire recovery and restoration efforts.
  • Aid in fire suppression activities by identifying hotspots and directing firefighting resources to areas most in need, optimizing resource allocation and minimizing response times.
  • Support efforts to mitigate the environmental impact of forest fires by monitoring fire behavior and facilitating targeted interventions to protect sensitive habitats and biodiversity.
